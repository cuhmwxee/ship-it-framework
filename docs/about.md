# About Ship It!

## Why

Ship It! was created to simplify software delivery.

It is not intended to replace existing methods such as Scrum, Kanban, or other team practices. Its purpose is to define the minimum workflow required to deliver validated software.

---

## Background

The framework was developed from practical software development experience.

It was not developed from academic research. It values practical experience before theory and aims to stay useful in everyday delivery work.

---

## Inspiration

The idea was partly inspired by Dave Thomas and a presentation that included the phrase "Shut up and ship!"

That phrase helped frame the question of what the smallest useful workflow for delivery might be. The inspiration is distinct from the framework itself. Ship It! was developed independently.

---

## Why publish this?

Ship It! may not solve your problems.

It may not fit your team.

It may not even tell you anything new.

But if it helps your team ask better questions about software delivery,
or gives you a simpler way to discuss an existing process,
then it has already served its purpose.

---

## Independent Development

Ship It! was developed independently.

Similarities to existing frameworks, terminology, or methods may appear because software engineering often converges on similar ideas. No existing framework was intentionally copied.

---

## Non-goals

Ship It! is intentionally small.

It does not attempt to define project management, planning, estimation, implementation details, or specific tools. For the normative definition of the framework, see [specification.md](specification.md).
